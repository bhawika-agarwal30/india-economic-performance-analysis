# Analysing India’s Economic Performance: Agriculture, Development, and Structural Gaps

## Overview
This project evaluates India’s economic performance with a focus on agriculture, productivity, employment, and structural development. It compares India’s performance with high-income countries to identify major development gaps and policy challenges.

## Objectives
- Assess India’s agricultural performance using macroeconomic indicators
- Compare India with high-income economies on productivity and sectoral contribution
- Identify structural issues affecting growth and development
- Evaluate the role of policy interventions in shaping economic outcomes

## Methodology
- Analysed macroeconomic and sectoral datasets
- Conducted comparative analysis between India and high-income countries
- Examined indicators such as agricultural value added, employment, productivity, and land use
- Interpreted long-term development trends and policy implications

## Key Insights
- India’s agricultural employment share remains high while productivity per worker remains low
- Structural issues such as disguised unemployment continue to affect efficiency
- India has a higher dependence on agriculture relative to high-income economies
- Policy interventions improved food production, but major development gaps still persist

## Tools Used
- Microsoft Excel
- Data Analysis
- Comparative Economic Analysis
- Policy Interpretation
